Chiral Effective Field Theory for Beta Decays (CBD) Collaboration

Introduction

This project aims to develop a theoretical framework for investigating weak decays in medium-heavy deformed nuclei based on nuclear forces and weak decay operators derived from chiral effective field theory and utilizes renormalization group methods. By integrating ab initio approaches with traditional nuclear structure models, the project seeks to compute nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for key nuclear weak decays and quantify their uncertainties. The overarching goal is to provide theoretical support for the search of new physics at the nuclear energy scales (e.g., CKM matrix unitarity and the effective neutrino mass) and the understanding of element formation in the Universe. Specifically, the project aims to achieve the following objectives:

1) Extend the In-Medium Renormalization Group (IMSRG) Method:

  • Derive chiral nuclear forces and weak decay operators evolved to the full single-particle space and valence-space configurations with a few major shells.
  • Apply these evolved interactions and operators to traditional nuclear structure models (pn-QRPA, PGCM, and PSM) to study nuclear weak decays.
    • 2) Develop an In-Medium Random Phase Approximation (IM-pn-QRPA, IM-pn-SRPA) Based on Chiral Nuclear Forces:
  • Compute single beta-decay rates of key nuclei involved in the rapid neutron capture process (r-process).
  • Determine nuclear matrix elements and their uncertainties for candidate nuclei undergoing neutrinoless double-beta decay.
  • Quantify the contributions of higher-order operators and many-body correlations to the axial-vector coupling constant.
    • 3) Construct Quantum Number Projection Methods (PGCM, PSM) Based on Chiral Nuclear Forces:
  • Assess the impact of quasiparticle excitations on the NMEs of key nuclear decays.
  • Provide nuclear matrix elements for superallowed Fermi transitions, improving the understanding of nuclear structure effects and isospin symmetry breaking in CKM matrix unitarity tests.
  • Determine the weak decay rates of key nuclei in astrophysical environments and quantify their uncertainties.
